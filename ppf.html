How This PPF Calculator Works

Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a long-term, tax-efficient scheme backed by the Government of India. You can invest up to a maximum limit each financial year. This calculator assumes a fixed yearly contribution and a constant interest rate compounded annually.

Approximate method used:
The balance at the end of each year is:
Balanceyear = (Previous Balance + Yearly Contribution) × (1 + r)
where r is the annual PPF interest rate (in decimal).

The growth chart compares how much you have actually invested vs the estimated account balance over the years. The doughnut chart at the bottom shows how much of the final maturity amount is your own contribution vs interest earned.

PPF rules (lock-in period, extension, partial withdrawal, loan facility, interest revisions) are defined by the Government of India and may change over time. Use this only as a planning tool and check official PPF rules or consult a financial advisor before investing.
