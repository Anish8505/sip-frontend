About Fixed Deposit (FD)

Bank FDs are low-risk instruments that give a fixed return for a fixed tenure. This calculator assumes yearly compounding to estimate maturity value.

Formula used (FD growth):
Maturity Value = Principal × (1 + R)n
where R is annual interest rate (in decimal) and n is number of years.

The line chart shows how your FD value grows each year compared to your original principal. The doughnut chart splits the final maturity amount into principal vs interest earned, so you can clearly see how much extra you earned.

Bank rules for compounding frequency, tax deducted at source (TDS) and premature withdrawal penalties may differ. Treat this as an educational estimate only and confirm exact numbers with your bank.
