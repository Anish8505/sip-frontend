How Recurring Deposits Work

In an RD you deposit a fixed amount every month. The bank pays interest on the accumulated balance. This calculator approximates monthly compounding to show how your corpus grows year by year.

Formula used (approximate monthly compounding):
Each monthly deposit grows at a monthly rate r = annual rate / 12 / 100 until maturity.
Future Value ≈ monthly deposit × ((1 + r)n − 1) / r × (1 + r),
where n is number of months.

The growth chart compares how much you have actually deposited vs the estimated maturity value over time. The doughnut chart splits the final amount into total deposits vs interest earned, so you can clearly see the benefit of compounding.

Exact RD formulas and interest credit frequency can differ between banks and Post Office schemes. Treat this as an educational tool, not a legal or tax document.
