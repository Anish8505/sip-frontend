What is a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a way of investing a fixed amount regularly into mutual funds or other investment products. Instead of putting a big amount in one shot, you invest monthly (or weekly/quarterly) and let compounding work over time.

For example, investing â‚¹5,000 every month for 10 years at 12% expected return can grow into several lakhs. Our SIP calculator helps you quickly estimate this maturity value and the profit over your invested amount.

How this SIP calculator works

  • Monthly Investment: How much you want to invest every month.
  • Expected Return (%): Average annual return you expect from your investment.
  • Time Period (years): How long you will continue the SIP.

Behind the scenes, the calculator uses the standard SIP formula with monthly compounding to show: Invested Amount, Maturity Amount and Total Profit.

What is a Lumpsum Investment?

A lumpsum investment is a one-time amount that you invest at once instead of spreading it across months. For example, investing â‚¹5,00,000 today and leaving it for 10 years at an expected return of 12% per year.

Our lumpsum calculator shows how much that single investment can grow over time, so you can compare SIP vs lumpsum for your financial goals.

When should you use SIP vs Lumpsum?

  • Choose SIP if you get a monthly salary and want to invest regularly.
  • Choose Lumpsum if you received a bonus, inheritance or have surplus cash.
  • You can also combine both: lumpsum at the start + SIP every month.
