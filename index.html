What is a SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly (usually monthly) in mutual funds. It helps average out your cost and builds wealth over time using the power of compounding.

How our SIP calculator helps you

  • Enter monthly investment amount.
  • Enter expected annual return rate.
  • Enter how many years you plan to invest.

The calculator instantly shows your total invested amount, maturity value and total profit based on the SIP formula with monthly compounding.

What is Lumpsum Investment?

In a lumpsum investment, you invest a one-time amount and let it grow for a number of years at a certain expected return. This is common when you receive a bonus, inheritance or sell an asset.

When should you choose SIP vs Lumpsum?

  • Choose SIP if you have regular monthly income and want disciplined investing.
  • Choose Lumpsum if you have surplus cash ready to invest immediately.
  • Many investors use both: one-time lumpsum + ongoing SIP.

What is an FD (Fixed Deposit)?

A Fixed Deposit is a safe investment where you deposit a lump sum with a bank for a fixed tenure at a fixed interest rate. In India, many FDs compound interest quarterly.

Our FD calculator estimates your invested amount, maturity amount and interest earned using quarterly compounding, based on your principal, interest rate and time period.

What is an RD (Recurring Deposit)?

A Recurring Deposit is similar to SIP in a bank: you deposit a fixed amount every month for a fixed tenure, and earn interest on your growing balance.

Our RD calculator helps you see how your monthly deposits grow over time, and how much interest you earn by the end of the RD tenure.

What is an EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)?

EMI is the fixed amount you pay every month towards a loan such as a home loan, car loan or personal loan. Each EMI contains both interest and principal repayment.

Our EMI calculator shows your monthly EMI, total amount paid over the entire tenure and the total interest you will pay on your loan, based on your loan amount, interest rate and tenure.
