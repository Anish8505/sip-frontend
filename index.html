Investment, Tax & Retirement Calculators in One Place

This page brings together SIP, Lumpsum, FD, RD, PPF, EMI, Income Tax, CAGR, Retirement, NPS and SWP calculators so you can quickly check how your money grows, what EMI you will pay, how much tax you may owe, how your retirement corpus might look and how long withdrawals can last.

SIP & Lumpsum

SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds, while lumpsum is a one-time investment. Both rely on compounding returns over time to grow your wealth.

FD, RD, PPF & NPS

Fixed Deposit (FD) is a safe investment at a fixed interest rate. Recurring Deposit (RD) lets you invest monthly at a bank. Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a tax-efficient, government-backed long-term scheme. National Pension System (NPS) is a retirement-focused product where you invest regularly for the long term and receive a mix of lump sum and pension at retirement.

EMI & Income Tax

EMI is the fixed amount you pay each month towards a home loan, car loan or personal loan. The income tax calculator uses simple Indian slabs for Old and New regimes plus 4% health & education cess to estimate your tax, net income after tax and effective tax rate.

CAGR, Retirement Corpus, NPS & SWP

CAGR shows your average annual growth rate between a starting and ending value. Retirement calculators help you estimate how much corpus you may need based on inflation and expenses. NPS calculators estimate how much your monthly retirement contributions can grow to. SWP calculators help you understand how long your corpus can last if you withdraw a fixed amount every month after retirement.

Important Disclaimer

All calculators on this page provide approximate values based on the inputs you provide and simplified formulas. Actual returns, loan EMIs, tax liabilities, retirement needs and withdrawal durations may differ based on market conditions, bank or fund policies, tax rules, deductions, surcharges and your actual lifestyle. Always verify with official sources or a qualified financial advisor before making final financial decisions.
