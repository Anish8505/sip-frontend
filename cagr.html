What Is CAGR?

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) tells you the constant annual growth rate of an investment from its starting value to its ending value, assuming it grows at a steady rate every year.

Formula:
CAGR = (Final / Initial)(1 / n) − 1
where n is the number of years.

Use CAGR to compare mutual funds, stocks or any long-term investments on a like-for-like basis.

CAGR Calculator for Indian Investors

This CAGR calculator helps you quickly understand how fast your money has grown each year between two points in time. Instead of only looking at total return, CAGR shows the smooth annualised growth rate which is easier to compare across different investments and time periods.

You can use it for mutual funds, stocks, SIP portfolios, real estate or any asset where you know the starting value, ending value and the number of years.
